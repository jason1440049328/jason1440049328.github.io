Automated Aircraft Damage Inspection and Tape Bubble Study

MAY 2019 - AUG 2019

Shortly after military aircraft are landed, the maintenance team often discover small pockets of air entrapped under tape betwteen aircraft parts. Current solutions require maintainers to deflate small air bubbles by cutting the tape or sometimes stripping and replacing tape over the entire aircraft.


Automatic Skunk Feeder

AUG 2018 - DEC 2018

We designed a fully portable and modular automatic feeder capable of dispensing food for small exhibit animals. At random time intervals throughout the day, food would be dispensed to simulate feeding in the wild without the need of a caretaker.

Engineered Microbes to Sense and Destroy Biofilms

MAY 2018 - NOV 2018

Biodiesel storage tanks are susceptible to water infiltration that often results in the formation of biofilms containing bacteria and fungi. Biofilms may clog pipes, degrade fuel, or corrode storage tanks. We set out to engineer a “seek, aim, and destroy” system for the remediation of microbial biofilm.

Engineered Microbes to Sense and Detect ETEC

MAY 2017 - NOV 2017

Every year, Enterotixigenic Escherichia coli (ETEC), the most common form of traveler's diarrhea, affects thousands of deployed warfighters. The goal is to engineer non-pathogenic E. coli to sense ETEC, response to it's presense, and package it in a cellulose matrix.
