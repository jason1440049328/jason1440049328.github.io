Sun Tracking Power Supply

JUN 2020 - AUG 2020

Group Members

  • Joshua Szymanski
  • Kevin McKleroy

Mentors

  • Mr. Ryan Wolfarth

The Task

Certain projects at AFLCMC require a Raspberry Pi, and groups working on those projects often remotely deploy their module to collect data. The Sun Tracker was built to support their remote deployment of a Raspberry Pi module without the need for daily human interaction. Ideally, our Sun Tracker would be entirely self-sufficient and capable of tracking and following the sun daily. To generate power, a 17W solar panel would be used in conjunction with a solar charge regulator to charge a battery and support both the Arduino module and Raspberry Pi.

Our Approach

  • Identified the wattage necessary for the solar panel to generate enough power for the battery, Raspberry Pi, and Arduino and made sure voltage was safe to charge the 12V battery.
  • Select a parts list and motors capable of inducing motion for turning and tilting towards the sun
  • Aquired Stepper Motor, Repurposd Drill Motor, Bumper Switches, Reed Switches, SPST Relays, DPDT Relays, and Photoresistors
  • Developed and tested logic for using photoresistor analog logic to allow Arduino to control motors accordingly
